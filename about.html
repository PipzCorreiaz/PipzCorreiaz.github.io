About Me

I am a researcher at GAIPS (INESC-ID) and a PhD student aiming at merging the topics I like the most: Human-Robot Interaction (HRI), Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Games. My main interest is to explore effective interactions with social robots in entertainment scenarios. My most recent (yet unexplored) ideas are also related with Interactive Task Learning (ITL) for game domains. The ideia of generalizing (computational) knowledge will allow us to produce much more complex programs, machines, and robots, which I truly believe it is the next big step for AI after machine learning.

After I received the undergraduate degree in Computer Science and Engineering (2013), I had a first experience at a research lab as a summer intern (GAIPS). Quickly I understood how social robotics could combine my background in Computer Science with the Social Sciences, which I found facinating. One year later, while I was doing my masters degree, again in Computer Science and Engineering, I did another summer intership at GAIPS (2014). Then, to finish the degree (2015), I accepted doing a master thesis supervised by Prof. Ana Paiva and Prof. Francisco S. Melo where I have created a social robot that plays the portuguese card game "Sueca".

In the following year I collaborated in the last 3 months of the EMOTE EU Project and the beginning of the AMIGOS Project. This year was a great opportunity for me to grow as a researcher and to confirm my intention to persue the PhD.
